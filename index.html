— 01 / The Problem

Food moves.
Visibility doesn't.

Today's distribution chains rely on paper logs, fragmented spreadsheets, and manual barcode scans that government staff simply do not have the bandwidth to perform. The result: silent leakage, delayed alerts, and no single source of truth for the people accountable for it.

Visibility Gap

Blind between handoffs

Once a truck leaves a mill, the bag effectively disappears from the system until it surfaces — sometimes — at a regional warehouse.

Capture Gap

Scanning that won't happen

Asking warehouse staff to scan thousands of bags per shift fails in practice. Automation is not a nice-to-have — it is the only viable path.

Decision Gap

Reports that arrive late

By the time a commissioner sees a monthly summary, leakage is already weeks old and the trail has gone cold.

— 02 / The Chain

Five stages.
One ledger.

Every bag — empty or filled — is born with an identity at the mill and accumulates a verifiable history at every gate it passes through, until it reaches the beneficiary.

01 · PROCUREMENT

From the farmer

Grain aggregated at country-specific collection points; suppliers logged at source.

02 · MILLING

Tagged at the line

Conveyor scanners assign a unique RFID/barcode identity to each filled bag at packaging.

03 · CENTRAL WAREHOUSE

Bulk in, ledgered

RFID gates auto-capture inbound pallets; inventory updates without touching a clipboard.

04 · REGIONAL HUB

Routed & geo-fenced

GPS-bonded shipments validated against destination; route deviations flagged in seconds.

05 · LAST MILE

Delivered to families

Mobile bulk-scan or AI vision confirms handoff at the distribution outlet.

EMPTY BAGS

Issued, returned, reused.

Sacks issued to mills carry their own identity. The system tracks issuance, fill events, and return cycles — surfacing reuse efficiency and inventory shrinkage that today goes invisible.

FILLED BAGS

Born at the mill, retired at the family.

Each filled bag carries a complete, tamper-evident lifecycle: when, where, by whom, and through which gates — until it reaches the beneficiary it was procured for.

— 03 / The Capture Layer

Zero-touch by
default.

Government capacity for manual scanning is limited. Our principle: if a human has to remember to scan it, the data won't arrive. Capture happens automatically — at gates, on conveyors, in vehicles, and through the camera lens.

RFID Gate Arrays

Drive-through portals at warehouse entry/exit auto-detect every tagged bag on the pallet — no operator action required.

CAPTURE · GATE

Conveyor Scanners

Fixed barcode tunnels at the mill's packaging line tag each bag the moment it is sealed and weighed.

CAPTURE · PROCESSING

Mobile Bulk Scanning

Camera-based AI recognizes dozens of bag tags from a single phone snapshot — built for last-mile field staff.

CAPTURE · FIELD

GPS Convoy Binding

Vehicles broadcast position every minute; geo-fences validate that shipments arrive where — and when — they should.

CAPTURE · TRANSIT

IoT Scan Tunnels

Sealed scanning chambers at hub transitions provide uninterrupted bag-by-bag verification at line speed.

CAPTURE · HUB

Integration APIs

Open APIs bridge the platform to existing ministry ERPs, WFP systems, and donor reporting pipelines.

CAPTURE · SYSTEM

— 05 / Intelligence

An AI layer that
writes the report
for you.

The platform doesn't just collect data — it interprets it. Predictive models, anomaly detection, and natural-language summaries turn raw scans into the briefings commissioners actually read.

  • i.

    Predictive analytics

    Demand forecasting by region, replenishment recommendations, and seasonal supply planning calibrated to historic distribution patterns.

  • ii.

    Anomaly detection

    Surfaces unusual losses, suspected diversion, and abnormal movement patterns that human reviewers would never spot in volume data.

  • iii.

    Intelligent alerts

    Push notifications for delayed shipments, low stock, and elevated damage rates — routed to the official accountable for that link in the chain.

  • iv.

    Natural-language reporting

    "Region X has a 15% increase in losses this month." Briefings written in plain language, generated on demand or on schedule.

  • v.

    Decision support

    Concrete recommendations: optimized routes, stock reallocations, and operational adjustments — ranked by projected impact.

"Region Borno shows a 15% rise in in-transit losses this month, concentrated on the R-19 → Maiduguri corridor. Recommend reassigning two convoys to the alternate northern route — projected loss reduction ~62% within four weeks."
